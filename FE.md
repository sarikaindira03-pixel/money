🌟 The Core App Philosophy (For Onboarding/Landing Page)
"This isn't a traditional tracking app that just sighs at your spending. It is a Vault-Driven Financial Engine. Your money lives in a core Main Vault, and you route it into color-coded 'smart envelopes' that have distinct behaviors depending on how strict or fluid you want that budget category to be."

🎨 The 4 Budget Colors: How to Explain Them to Users
🔸 Orange | The "Unexpected" Envelope
What it is: A pure emergency or spur-of-the-moment expense category (e.g., Sudden Medical Bill, Car Breakdown).

How it works: You don't set a budget for this at the start of the month. It always starts at $0. When you spend money here, the app instantly pulls it straight out of your Main Vault. Your "Budgeted" amount dynamically grows to perfectly match your "Spent" amount so you never show a deficit.

User Boundary: You cannot pre-allocate or save money into an Orange bucket. It is reactive.

🟡 Yellow | The "Fluid" Envelope
What it is: For categories that are highly predictable but variable month-to-month (e.g., Groceries, Utilities).

How it works: You set a starting budget (e.g., $200).

If you save money: The leftover amount doesn't stay trapped; it is automatically swept back up and added straight back into your Main Vault at the end of the tracking cycle to keep your main cash pool healthy.

If you overspend: The app automatically pulls the extra cash from your Main Vault to cover you.

User Boundary: Your budget limits are flexible. The Main Vault acts as your safety net and your savings bin.

🔴🟢 Red & Green | The "Pocket Money" Envelopes
What it is: Hand-off cash for discretionary, guilt-free spending (e.g., 🟢 Hobbies/Gifts, 🔴 Personal Splurges/Entertainment).

How it works: You allocate a set amount (e.g., $100).

If you save money: The app stops tracking accountability for the leftovers. That money stays in your physical or digital pocket! The app will never take it back or put it back in the Main Vault. It belongs entirely to you.

If you overspend: The app steps in as an emergency fallback and pulls the deficit directly from your Main Vault.

User Boundary: Underspending here results in "invisible" cash. The system assumes you walked away with it in your physical pocket.

🔹 Blue | The "Isolated Goals" Envelope
What it is: For self-contained, long-term sinking funds or irregular goals (e.g., Vacation Fund, Yearly Insurance Premium, Tech Upgrades).

How it works: These buckets do not interact with the Main Vault day-to-day. They interact with a dedicated Blue Vault.

If you save money: The leftover money rolls over and piles up inside your private Blue Vault for future use.

If you overspend: The system draws the extra cash strictly from your accumulated Blue Vault balance, not your Main Vault.

User Boundary (Hard Limit): The Hard Stop. If your Blue Vault is out of money, you cannot overspend your Blue allocation. The app will throw a strict error and block the entry.
